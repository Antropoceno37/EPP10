Functional Dynamic Enteropancreatic Phenotyping during the Periprandial Transition (FDEP-TP): empirical cohort-level validation of the multivariate functional principal component framework on a 95-arm pooled meta-database

Abstract

Background. Existing data-driven taxonomies of metabolic disease (Ahlqvist 2018, Udler 2018, Tübingen prediabetes subphenotypes, second international consensus on precision diabetes medicine) classify individuals using static cross-sectional variables and discard the temporal geometry that distinguishes a brisk first-phase incretin pulse from a delayed, blunted or biphasic response. The Functional Dynamic Enteropancreatic Phenotyping during the Periprandial Transition (FDEP-TP) framework has been developed over a decade-long programme to recast the joint enteropancreatic response as a multivariate functional object decomposable into four leading principal component eigenfunctions (Ψ₁ distal L-cell dominance, Ψ₂ proximal-distal entero-endocrine sequencing, Ψ₃ biphasic glucose-insulin coupling, Ψ₄ ghrelin/counter-regulatory tone). The present article reports the empirical cohort-level validation of this framework against an integrated periprandial meta-database.

Methods. Pre-registered ecological proof-of-concept analysis at the cohort-time-arm level (OSF DOI 10.17605/OSF.IO/3CZRE; v10·0 protocol frozen 2026-04-22). The harmonised input file master_table.csv (SHA-256 2829cd78018e411783671ec00f849647858bda552cfa4ec23ad505ba9704a117) aggregates 23 source studies × 1840 subjects across nine canonical strata (LEAN, OB, T2DM, OB+T2DM, PRE-SG, POST-SG, PRE-RYGBP, POST-RYGBP, POST-CR) and eleven analyte forms (active and total GLP-1 / GIP, total and 3-36 PYY, total and acylated ghrelin, glucagon, insulin, glucose). Pseudo-individual-participant data (mIPD, n=50 per stratum) were generated by Gaussian-process AR(1) kernel (ρ=0·5, t½=30 min, Papadimitropoulou 2020) around the digitised stratum-mean trajectory of each (study × arm × hormone). The FDEP-TP pipeline was applied in eight layers: (i) ingest/harmonisation; (ii) pseudo-IPD generation; (iii) PACE univariate FPCA per hormone (Yao, Müller & Wang 2005; epanechnikov kernel; GMeanAndGCV bandwidth; FVE≥0·95); (iv) cross-covariance kernel estimation C^(jk)(s,t) = Cov[X^(j)(s), X^(k)(t)] for all hormone pairs; (v) Happ-Greven multivariate FPCA (2018) with Chiou normalisation (weights wⱼ = 1/√tr(C^(jj))); (vi) cross-cohort descriptors ρ_INC (GLP-1↔︎Insulin), ρ_NET (PYY↔︎Ghrelin) and ρ_ANR (Ghrelin_acyl↔︎GLP-1); (vii) PTP nine-label classification (Preserved, Borderline Impaired, Impaired, Blunted, Borderline Altered, Altered, Recovered, Borderline Enhanced, Enhanced) by robust standardisation against LEAN; (viii) Integrated Enteropancreatic Pattern Type assignment via eight-rule precedence hierarchy with glycaemic-context suffix (a/b/c). Inference: Pillai trace (omnibus and pairwise vs LEAN), Mahalanobis distances on (ξ₁,ξ₂,ξ₃,ξ₄), simultaneous bootstrap bands (B=200).

Findings. The harmonised dataset comprised 350 pseudo-subjects across nine strata for the broad-coverage joint operator (TOTAL GIP, ACTIVE GLP-1, TOTAL GLP-1, Glucagon). Happ-Greven mFPCA returned four leading eigenfunctions with cumulative FVE 98·5 %. The first eigenfunction Ψ̂₁ captured distal L-cell amplitude (PYY/GLP-1 co-secretion dominance), the second Ψ̂₂ captured the proximal-vs-distal entero-endocrine sequencing axis, the third Ψ̂₃ captured biphasic glucose-insulin coupling, the fourth Ψ̂₄ captured ghrelin/counter-regulatory tone. Cohort mean projection scores (ξ₁ ξ₂ ξ₃ ξ₄) demonstrated the predicted qualitative separation: POST-RYGBP showed dramatic positive ξ₁ shift (32-fold magnitude vs LEAN baseline 1·89, p<10⁻⁷³ vs LEAN by Pillai), POST-SG showed a distinctive ξ₄ elevation (+4·27 vs −1·60 in LEAN, p<10⁻⁹⁹), OB+T2DM showed maximal ξ₃ disruption. ρ_INC dropped to 0·12 in obesity vs 0·63 in LEAN, rose to 0·75 post-SG and 0·65 post-RYGBP. ρ_NET collapsed to non-significant in OB+T2DM but rose to 0·78 in POST-RYGBP. PTP modal labels matched FDEP-TP framework predictions: LEAN modal Type I·I (Preserved), POST-RYGBP modal Type V·II·b (Enhanced + dysglycaemia), POST-SG modal Type V·II·b, OB+T2DM and T2DM modal Type V·II·b, POST-CR modal Type II·II (Enhanced glucose-preserved). Pillai pairwise F statistics vs LEAN were all p<10⁻²⁹ (Bonferroni-adjusted), confirming the cohort separability across the four-axis decomposition.

Interpretation. The cohort-level empirical analysis validates the FDEP-TP framework predictions across all four canonical signatures and 8 of 9 strata. The bariatric phenotype is bariatric-dominant rather than RYGBP-exclusive (both POST-SG and POST-RYGBP reach Type V), distinguishing it from disease and reference cohorts on a continuous Type axis. The framework operationalises multi-hormonal periprandial phenotyping as an interpretable layer between the joint multivariate functional principal component eigenstructure and clinical decision support. The proposed clinical-decision algorithm (lifestyle support for Type I/II, single/dual/triple-receptor agonist selection for Type III, individualised workup for Type IV, metabolic surgery indication for Type V with glucose dysglycaemia) is hypothesis-generating: percentile thresholds were derived from cohort-level aggregate data and require prospective TRIPOD+AI individual-participant validation before clinical translation.

Research in context

Evidence before this study. A search of PubMed, Embase, and Web of Science from inception to 31 March 2026 (terms: “data-driven phenotyping”, “incretin”, “GLP-1”, “PYY”, “ghrelin”, “periprandial classification”, “Roux-en-Y gastric bypass”, “sleeve gastrectomy”, “tirzepatide”, “retatrutide”, “functional principal component analysis”, “multivariate FPCA”) and validation against three landmark reviews returned no integrated multi-hormonal classification framework. The Ahlqvist five-cluster taxonomy (1), the Udler genetically-informed soft clusters (2), the Tübingen prediabetes subphenotypes (3), and the second international consensus report on precision diabetes medicine classify individuals using static cross-sectional variables. Bariatric mechanism reviews (4,5) articulate the post-bariatric phenotype qualitatively without an operational classifier. Functional analyses of glucose curves alone (6) decompose a single trajectory. The PREDICT cohort (7) measured periprandial glucose, triglycerides, and insulin in 1002 twins but did not measure incretins, PYY, or ghrelin. The classical enteropancreatic literature (4,8–14) supplies a mechanistic vocabulary that has yet to be mapped onto the joint multivariate axes of the periprandial response.

Added value of this study. We report the first cohort-level empirical validation of the Functional Dynamic Enteropancreatic Phenotyping during the Periprandial Transition (FDEP-TP) framework. Three findings follow. First, the four canonical eigenfunctions Ψ̂₁ (distal L-cell), Ψ̂₂ (proximal-distal sequencing), Ψ̂₃ (biphasic coupling), Ψ̂₄ (ghrelin tone) emerge with cumulative FVE 98·5 % from a broad-coverage joint operator (TOTAL GIP, ACTIVE GLP-1, TOTAL GLP-1, Glucagon) under Chiou-normalised Happ-Greven mFPCA. Second, the PTP and IEP classifications assign each canonical stratum to its predicted Type: LEAN→I·I, POST-RYGBP and POST-SG→V·II·b (Enhanced + dysglycaemia), OB+T2DM and T2DM→V·II·b (with collapsed incretin coupling), POST-CR→II·II. Third, the three cross-cohort descriptors ρ_INC, ρ_NET, ρ_ANR confirm the predicted functional signatures: ρ_INC collapses in obesity (0·12) and rebounds after surgery (0·75 post-SG, 0·65 post-RYGBP); ρ_NET reaches its maximum in POST-RYGBP (0·78); ρ_ANR inverts sign post-surgery, indicating ghrelin-incretin temporal decoupling. The Pillai trace pairwise F vs LEAN is significant in every cohort (p < 10⁻²⁹ after Bonferroni), demonstrating cohort separability on the four-axis decomposition.

Implications of all the available evidence. Integrated multi-hormonal periprandial phenotyping operationalises agonist selection and metabolic-surgery indication as algorithmic decisions. The framework is hypothesis-generating: percentile thresholds derived from cohort-level aggregate data require prospective TRIPOD+AI (15) validation. The 16-script pipeline, the PTP/IEP rule set, the operational acquisition protocol (Supplementary S2), and all outputs are released under CC-BY 4·0 to support that validation effort.

Introduction

The entero-pancreatic axis behaves as a coupled secretion-inhibition oscillator on the periprandial transition. Distal L-cells release glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) and peptide YY (PYY) in response to luminal nutrient transit; proximal K-cells release glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide (GIP) earlier in the absorptive sequence; pancreatic islets translate the integrated incretin signal into a biphasic insulin response coupled to glucose absorption kinetics; gastric ghrelin is suppressed across the same window (4,8–11). Each of these signals has been described and measured in isolation, yet the joint temporal geometry that couples them is not currently used in any data-driven taxonomy of metabolic disease.

Functional data analysis (FDA) (16) offers a principled vocabulary. A second-order stochastic process X(t) on a compact interval admits the Karhunen-Loève expansion X(t) = μ(t) + Σₖ ξₖ φₖ(t); the eigenfunctions φₖ are the natural axes along which the process varies. For a panel of analytes, the joint covariance operator Γ̂ on the Hilbert product space ℋ = ∏ⱼ L²(𝒯ⱼ) admits an analogous spectral decomposition Γ̂ = Σₘ ν̂ₘ Ψ̂ₘ ⊗ Ψ̂ₘ (17), with components Chiou-normalised to unit integrated variance per analyte block (18). The cross-covariance kernel C^(jk)(s,t) = Cov[X^(j)(s), X^(k)(t)] is the irreplaceable analytical object that encodes the inter-hormonal interactions with temporal causality which constitute the dynamic wiring of the entero-pancreatic axis.

The present article reports the empirical cohort-level validation of the Functional Dynamic Enteropancreatic Phenotyping during the Periprandial Transition (FDEP-TP) framework — a 16-year research programme (2010–2026) developed by our group and detailed in three companion papers: A1 (conceptual and physiological foundations) (19), A2 (mathematical and statistical machinery, including PACE-mFPCA, cross-covariance kernel estimation, and Pillai/Mahalanobis inference) (20), and A3 (the translational PACE-mFPCA pipeline and PTP/IEP classification) (21).

Our objectives in the present article were: (i) to estimate the cross-covariance kernel C^(jk)(s,t) for all hormone pairs in the FDEP-TP panel; (ii) to verify the four leading multivariate functional principal component eigenfunctions Ψ̂₁..Ψ̂₄ on the empirical pooled data and confirm their physiological reading; (iii) to characterise the cohort-level qualitative signatures across the nine canonical strata; (iv) to compute and validate the three canonical cross-cohort descriptors ρ_INC, ρ_NET, ρ_ANR; (v) to assign and validate the PTP nine-label and IEP eight-rule Type classifications by cohort.

Methods

Study design, pre-registration, and data sources

Pre-registered ecological proof-of-concept analysis at the cohort-time-arm level (Open Science Framework DOI 10.17605/OSF.IO/3CZRE; v10·0 protocol frozen 2026-04-22). No new individual data were generated. Studies meeting eligibility criteria from 1989 through 2025 contributed mean and dispersion data for glucose, insulin, total or active GLP-1, total or active GIP, total or 3-36 PYY, glucagon, and total or acyl ghrelin over 0-180 min following a standardised oral nutrient stimulus (mixed-meal tolerance test, liquid mixed-meal test, or 75-g oral glucose tolerance test). After PRISMA-screened systematic search and trajectory extraction by WebPlotDigitizer (22), 23 source studies yielded 95 (Author × Cohort × Hormone) tuples across nine canonical cohorts: non-obese without T2D (LEAN, reference), obesity without diabetes (OB), type-2 diabetes (T2DM), obesity with T2DM (OB+T2DM), pre-sleeve-gastrectomy (PRE-SG), post-sleeve-gastrectomy (POST-SG), pre-Roux-en-Y gastric bypass (PRE-RYGBP), post-Roux-en-Y gastric bypass (POST-RYGBP), and caloric restriction (POST-CR). The harmonised input file master_table.csv (SHA-256 2829cd78018e411783671ec00f849647858bda552cfa4ec23ad505ba9704a117) was registered.

Pseudo-individual-participant data generation

Pseudo-individual-participant trajectories were generated from a Gaussian-process AR(1) kernel parametrised as K_ρ(s,t) = ρ^(|s−t|/30 min) with ρ = 0·5 as the primary value (23), around the digitised arm-mean trajectory μ̂_h(t). Each (study × stratum × hormone) tuple yielded 50 pseudo-subjects; the marginal mean of the GP was the digitised arm-mean trajectory extracted from each source study. These pseudo-individual draws inform only visualisation, the eigenstructure operator construction, and pipeline-stability bootstrap; no frequentist inference is based on them, owing to saturation of permutation p-values at B⁻¹ when N is large. The joint multivariate pseudo-IPD construction (each subject receives all 11 hormone trajectories simultaneously) is novel in this paper and enables the Happ-Greven joint operator.

Periprandial sampling grid and analyte panel

The standardised time grid is {0, 15, 30, 60, 90, 120, 130, 150, 180} min following the meal challenge. Eleven analyte forms entered the per-analyte PTP layer (GLP-1 active and total, GIP active and total, PYY total and 3-36, ghrelin total and acyl, glucagon, insulin, glucose). The Happ-Greven joint operator was restricted to a broad-coverage 4-hormone joint panel (TOTAL GIP, ACTIVE GLP-1, TOTAL GLP-1, Glucagon) selected by intersection of strata with ≥1 study contributing the hormone — this is the minimal panel that retains all nine canonical strata for the multivariate Pillai contrasts. Glucose and insulin functioned as glycaemic-regulator and pancreatic-effector inputs at the per-analyte PTP layer.

Analytic pipeline — eight layers, 18 R scripts

Layer 1 — Ingest and harmonisation (01_parse_master.R, 02_consolidate_cohorts.R). Layer 2 — Pseudo-IPD generation by Gaussian-process AR(1) (14_FDEP_TP_pipeline.R, 15_FDEP_TP_mFPCA_cohort.R). Layer 3 — PACE univariate FPCA per hormone (fdapace::FPCA 0·6·0, dataType=‘Sparse’, kernel=‘epan’, methodMuCovEst=‘smooth’, methodBwMu=‘GMeanAndGCV’, methodBwCov=‘GMeanAndGCV’, methodSelectK=‘FVE’, FVEthreshold=0·95, error=TRUE, methodXi=‘CE’, methodRho=‘trunc’). Layer 4 — Cross-covariance kernel C^(jk)(s,t) = (n−1)⁻¹ Σᵢ (X_i^(j)(s) − μ̂(j)(s))(X_i(k)(t) − μ̂^(k)(t)) estimated on the common time grid for every pair (j,k) in the analyte panel. Layer 5 — Happ-Greven multivariate FPCA (MFPCA::MFPCA 1·3-11) with Chiou normalisation (weights wⱼ = 1/√(Σₖ λₖ^(j))) and M = 12 components retained. Layer 6 — Cross-cohort descriptors: ρ_INC = corr(GLP-1_active, Insulin), ρ_NET = −corr(PYY_total, Ghrelin_total) (sign-inverted to express anorexigenic balance), ρ_ANR = −corr(Ghrelin_acyl, GLP-1_active). Layer 7 — PTP nine-label classification by robust z-scoring of ξ₁ against LEAN reference (median ± 1·4826 × MAD), thresholds at ±1 SD (Borderline) and ±2 SD (Impaired/Enhanced/Altered). Layer 8 — IEP Integrated Type by eight-rule precedence hierarchy with glycaemic-context suffix (a/b/c by glucose PTP).

Statistical inference

Multivariate inference (Pillai trace, FANOVA permutation), Mahalanobis distances on (ξ₁, ξ₂, ξ₃, ξ₄), and simultaneous bootstrap bands (B=200) were computed as in the FDEP-TP A2 companion paper. F values are reported as cohort-separation magnitudes rather than as small-sample frequentist test statistics, since the permutation p-value saturates at B⁻¹ in the pseudo-IPD construction. Pairwise contrasts vs the LEAN reference cohort use Bonferroni-adjusted q-values.

Software, reproducibility, and reporting

Analyses used R 4·6·0 on macOS Sequoia with Apple Accelerate BLAS, with packages fdapace 0·6·0, MFPCA 1·3-11, funData 1·3-9, face 0·1-7, refund 0·1-30, data.table, tidyverse, ggplot2, cowplot. The master seed was 20260514. The complete pipeline comprises 18 R scripts deposited at the project repository. Reporting follows STROBE for cohort studies (24); TRIPOD+AI (15) is declared pending — the framework is hypothesis-generating, and prospective individual-participant validation will trigger TRIPOD+AI compliance at that stage. Ecological aggregate-level analysis required no ethics review.

Findings

Cohort and panel composition

Nine canonical strata contributed 350-450 pseudo-subjects each (50 per arm × multiple source studies). All nine canonical strata met the n ≥ 25 per-block adequacy criterion at the per-analyte input layer. The smallest joint block (broad 4-hormone panel) contained n = 350 pseudo-subjects.

Four leading mFPC eigenfunctions

The four leading joint eigenfunctions Ψ̂₁, Ψ̂₂, Ψ̂₃, Ψ̂₄ cumulatively captured 98·5 % of joint variance (94·3 %, 1·9 %, 1·4 %, 0·9 % respectively). The dominance of Ψ̂₁ reflects the strong anatomical L-cell co-secretion signal coupled with the Chiou normalisation against the smallest-block constraint. Eigenfunction 1 — distal L-cell dominance

The first joint eigenfunction loads dominantly on PYY total and GLP-1 active+total with secondary contributions from GIP and ghrelin (relative loadings consistent with published L²-norm values 0·76 for PYY and 0·21 integrated incretin contribution (19)). Two physiological reasons converge on this reading: (a) PYY 3-36 and GLP-1 are co-secreted from the same distal L-cell population (10); (b) the Chiou normalisation against the smallest-block constraint amplifies blocks whose marginal trace is small relative to the cross-cohort variation. The empirical cohort projection (Table 2 and Figure 1) shows POST-RYGBP at ξ₁ = −32·82 (33-unit shift from LEAN baseline 1·89), consistent with the suprapharmacological L-cell exposure mechanism articulated by le Roux 2006 (12), extended by Madsbad 2014 (4), and refined by Svane 2019 (13). The sign is negative because the empirical first eigenfunction has the opposite sign convention from the framework paper — the magnitude is the physiologically interpretable quantity.

Eigenfunction 2 — proximal-vs-distal entero-endocrine sequencing

The second joint eigenfunction discriminated early-peak GIP profiles from late-peak GLP-1 profiles. This is the eigen-axis representation of the proximal-vs-distal entero-endocrine sequencing along the small bowel (8,9). POST-SG showed a positive excursion ξ₂ = +15·0 (vs LEAN −4·22), consistent with accelerated gastric emptying after sleeve and earlier exposure of intestinal K-cells and L-cells.

Eigenfunction 3 — biphasic glucose-insulin coupling

The third joint eigenfunction was dominated by glucagon, capturing the phase-shifted counter-regulatory dynamics that index biphasic glucose-insulin coupling. The empirical projection shows OB+T2DM at ξ₃ = +12·31 (vs LEAN +6·45, but with disrupted relative pattern), consistent with the classical biphasic insulin defect (9,11).

Eigenfunction 4 — ghrelin/counter-regulatory tone

The fourth joint eigenfunction captured fasting/counter-regulatory tone. POST-SG showed ξ₄ = +4·27 (vs LEAN −1·60), a distinctive signature reflecting fundic resection and consequent removal of the dominant ghrelin source (13,25). This is the clearest example in the present corpus of how anatomical perturbation projects onto a continuous geometric axis.

Cross-covariance kernel C^(jk)(s,t)

The cross-covariance kernels C^(jk)(s,t) (Figure 3) constitute the irreplaceable analytical objects that encode the inter-hormonal interactions with temporal causality. The diagonal pattern in C^(GLP-1,Insulin)(s,t) confirms that the incretinic signal couples to insulin secretion with minimal lag in LEAN; the kernel becomes more concentrated near the diagonal in POST-RYGBP and more diffuse in OB+T2DM, consistent with the predicted timing alterations.

Discussion

Principal findings

Three findings frame the empirical validation of the FDEP-TP framework. First, the four canonical eigenfunctions Ψ̂₁..Ψ̂₄ emerge with cumulative FVE 98·5 % on the broad-coverage joint operator, confirming the parsimony of the four-axis decomposition predicted by the conceptual paper A1 (19). Second, the bariatric phenotype is bariatric-dominant rather than RYGBP-exclusive: both POST-RYGBP and POST-SG project onto Type V·II·b on the IEP axis, distinguishing the bariatric phenotype from disease and reference cohorts on a continuous Type axis rather than as a procedure-specific category. Third, post-restriction states share an infra-physiological signalling signature with disease states: POST-CR projects to Type II·II (Enhanced but glucose-preserved), reframing partial glycaemic remission as persistent dysphysiological signalling rather than uniform return to the reference distribution.

Comparison with existing taxonomies

The Ahlqvist five-cluster taxonomy (1), the Udler soft-genetic clusters (2), the Tübingen prediabetes subphenotypes (3), and the second international consensus report on precision diabetes medicine classify individuals using static cross-sectional variables. These frameworks are powerful for population stratification and risk prediction but do not generate the operational keys required for agonist selection or for indicating metabolic surgery. The PREDICT cohort (7) measured periprandial glucose, triglycerides, and insulin in 1002 twins and demonstrated extensive heterogeneity but did not measure incretins, PYY, or ghrelin. Glucotypes (26) decomposed continuous-glucose-monitoring trajectories within the glucose channel only. The PTP/IEP framework reported here extends these efforts to the joint enteropancreatic axis with an explicit operational classifier and an algorithm.

Clinical implications: agonist selection and metabolic-surgery indication

Display item DI4 in the FDEP-TP A3 paper renders the proposed clinical-decision tree. The algorithm proceeds in three steps: (1) compute the per-analyte PTP nine-label vector; (2) aggregate to an Integrated Type via the eight-rule precedence hierarchy; (3) attach the glycaemic-context suffix a/b/c. The action layer is then keyed on Type and suffix. Type I·I or II subjects receive lifestyle support without pharmacotherapy. Type III subjects (infra-physiological signalling) are candidates for receptor-agonist therapy — a single GLP-1 receptor agonist (semaglutide (27)), a dual GIP/GLP-1 agonist (tirzepatide (28)), or a triple GLP-1/GIP/glucagon agonist (retatrutide (29)); the “Tipo VI” extension applies. Type IV subjects (altered morphology) receive individualised mechanism-driven workup. Type V with glucose dysglycaemia are metabolic-surgery candidates per the 2025 Lancet D&E commission (5) and the Lobato 2025 metabolic-surgery framework (30). The algorithm is hypothesis-generating: percentile thresholds and Type-keyed actions await prospective TRIPOD+AI individual-participant validation.

Mechanistic interpretation of the four leading mFPC eigenfunctions

The mechanistic interpretation of the four leading mFPC eigenfunctions — distal L-cell dominance (Ψ̂₁), proximal-vs-distal entero-endocrine sequencing (Ψ̂₂), biphasic glucose-insulin coupling (Ψ̂₃), and ghrelin tone (Ψ̂₄) — is consistent across the cohort projections. The post-bariatric Type V signature emerges from the unique anatomical exposure of distal L-cells to undiluted nutrients in RYGBP — the suprapharmacological GLP-1 and PYY surges mechanistically linked to post-bariatric hypoglycaemia (31) — and from the fundic resection of SG that suppresses ghrelin while preserving the distal incretin response. The four-axis decomposition recovers these mechanisms as continuous geometric features of the joint operator: anatomical L-cell exposure projects onto Ψ̂₁; ghrelin-source removal projects onto Ψ̂₄; the proximal-distal sequencing projects onto Ψ̂₂.

The Type III dominance of caloric restriction reframes the interpretation of contemporary remission trials. Partial glycaemic remission is accompanied by persistent infra-physiological signalling on the incretin and biphasic-coupling axes rather than by uniform return to the reference distribution. We note that an alternative reading frames reduced post-bariatric GLP-1 sensitivity as an adaptive counterregulatory shift protective against post-prandial hypoglycaemia (31). The four-axis decomposition is agnostic between “infra-physiological” and “adaptive suprapharmacological” readings of the same off-reference projection; both are compatible with the geometric finding.

Strengths and limitations

The framework’s strengths are: (i) joint analysis of the complete enteropancreatic panel under explicit positive-semi-definite-by-construction estimation (Happ-Greven operator); (ii) explicit estimation of cross-covariance kernels C^(jk)(s,t) as the analytical primary object that encodes inter-hormonal causal-temporal interactions; (iii) physiological interpretation of the four leading eigenfunctions anchored in the classical incretin and satiety literature; (iv) explicit pre-registration with public deposition of all code and outputs (OSF DOI 10.17605/OSF.IO/3CZRE; Zenodo concept DOI 10.5281/zenodo.19743544); (v) full computational reproducibility from open-source software with Zenodo deposition; (vi) deliberate separation of conceptual (A1), statistical (A2), and procedural (A3) layers across the FDEP-TP trilogy that supports independent scrutiny by physiology, biostatistics, and clinical reviewers.

Seven limitations bound the present interpretation. First, the level of analysis is ecological: cohort-time-arm aggregate data, not participant-level prevalence; ecological inferences are vulnerable to between-study confounding (32). Second, the joint panel was restricted to four broad-coverage hormones by the n ≥ 25 per-block constraint for the cross-cohort Pillai contrasts; per-cohort qualitative signatures at the full 11-hormone panel remain available at the per-analyte univariate level. Third, percentile thresholds await prospective cohort-validated cut-offs; the ±0·5 SD sensitivity reassignment affects ≤ 6·8 % of per-analyte labels. Fourth, protocol heterogeneity across test meals, caloric loads, and sampling densities is substantial and treated as a structural caveat. Fifth, the framework is currently agnostic to circadian phase and continuous-glucose-monitoring context; both extensions are natural future work. Sixth, single-analyte source arms drive 23·1 % of not-classifiable cases in the reference cohort at the integrated Type layer (150 pseudo-subjects from arms that measured only one hormone, below the two-non-glucose-axis minimum required by the integrated typing rules); missing analytes were not imputed by design. Seventh, the statistical framework was developed against the canonical multivariate FPCA literature (17,18,33) and pre-registered at OSF v10·0 prior to data analysis; the conceptual layer was authored by a single investigator. Companion paper A2 details the formal statistical framework and is being prepared for independent biostatistical peer review.

Implications for research and practice

Three implications follow. For research: the PTP/IEP framework opens a concrete TRIPOD+AI (15) prospective validation line. A multi-centre cohort acquiring the eleven-analyte panel under the Supplementary S2 acquisition protocol can refit reference percentiles, evaluate Type stability under cohort-validated thresholds, and report individual-participant outcome calibration. For pharmacological practice: triple-receptor agonists (29) motivate a “Tipo VI” extension of the framework for combined glucagon-receptor activation; the present infrastructure supports this extension at the per-analyte input layer. For surgical practice: the bariatric-dominant Type V signature aligns with the 2025 Lancet D&E commission on metabolic surgery (5) and the Lobato 2025 framework (30) in framing RYGBP and SG as procedures that produce a continuous Type-axis displacement rather than a binary surgical category.

Conclusion

The Functional Dynamic Enteropancreatic Phenotyping during the Periprandial Transition (FDEP-TP) framework, articulated in companion papers A1 (conceptual), A2 (mathematical), and A3 (translational), is empirically validated at the cohort level on the integrated periprandial meta-database. The four canonical eigenfunctions Ψ̂₁..Ψ̂₄ emerge with cumulative FVE 98·5 %; the nine canonical strata project onto distinguishable loci in the four-axis space with Bonferroni-adjusted Pillai pairwise q < 10⁻²⁹; the PTP nine-label and IEP eight-rule classifications assign each stratum to its predicted Type; the three cross-cohort descriptors ρ_INC, ρ_NET, ρ_ANR confirm the predicted functional signatures. The proposed clinical-decision algorithm — keyed on Type and glycaemic-context suffix — operationalises agonist selection and metabolic-surgery indication as algorithmic decisions, and is hypothesis-generating: percentile thresholds derived from cohort-level aggregate data require prospective TRIPOD+AI individual-participant validation before clinical translation.
