Dear Editor,

I respectfully submit for consideration as an **Original Research article** the manuscript entitled:

> *"Functional Dynamic Enteropancreatic Phenotyping during the Periprandial Transition (FDEP-TP): empirical cohort-level validation of the multivariate functional principal component framework on a 95-arm pooled meta-database."*

This is the culminating empirical paper of the FDEP-TP trilogy that I have developed over a sixteen-year research programme (2010–2026). The conceptual foundations are reported in companion paper **A1** (currently under review at *Lancet Diabetes & Endocrinology*, Zenodo v3·0-A1 DOI 10.5281/zenodo.20102979); the mathematical and statistical machinery in companion paper **A2** (Zenodo v2·0-A2 DOI 10.5281/zenodo.20102959); the procedural and translational pipeline with PTP/IEP classification rules in companion paper **A3** (Zenodo v3·0-A3 DOI 10.5281/zenodo.20102987). The present article **empirically validates the framework predictions at the cohort level** using a 23-study, 1840-subject pooled meta-database with the full nine-canonical-strata coverage that prior work could not access.

**Why this paper is appropriate for *Lancet Diabetes & Endocrinology*.**

1. **A novel taxonomy keyed to multi-hormonal periprandial dynamics.** Existing data-driven taxonomies (Ahlqvist 2018, Udler 2018, Tübingen prediabetes subphenotypes, the second international consensus on precision diabetes medicine) classify individuals from static cross-sectional variables. The FDEP-TP framework operationalises a **continuous-geometry classifier** keyed on the joint trajectory of GLP-1, GIP, PYY, ghrelin, glucagon, insulin, and glucose across the periprandial transition.

2. **An operational clinical-decision algorithm.** The proposed three-step algorithm — compute per-analyte PTP nine-label vector → aggregate to Integrated Type via eight-rule precedence → attach glycaemic-context suffix — generates explicit decision keys for **lifestyle support, single/dual/triple receptor-agonist selection, and metabolic-surgery indication** (the latter aligned with the 2025 Lancet D&E commission on clinical obesity, Rubino et al.).

3. **Pre-registered, fully reproducible, open code.** The 18-script analytic pipeline, the harmonised master table (SHA-256 checksumed), the cross-covariance kernels C^(jk)(s,t), the four canonical eigenfunctions, the PTP/IEP rule set, and all numerical outputs are released under CC-BY 4·0 at Zenodo and GitHub. Pre-registration is at OSF DOI 10.17605/OSF.IO/3CZRE (v10·0 protocol frozen 2026-04-22). I declare TRIPOD+AI compliance as **pending** since the framework is hypothesis-generating and percentile thresholds derived from cohort-level aggregate data require prospective individual-participant validation before clinical translation.

4. **The bariatric-dominant Type V signature.** The empirical analysis confirms that the post-bariatric phenotype is **bariatric-dominant** rather than RYGBP-exclusive (both POST-SG and POST-RYGBP project onto Type V·II·b on the IEP axis), distinguishing it from disease and reference cohorts on a continuous Type axis. This finding has direct relevance for the 2025 Lancet D&E commission framework and the Lobato 2025 metabolic-surgery framework, both of which are cited.

5. **The cross-covariance kernel C^(jk)(s,t) as the analytical primary object.** The article is explicit that the systematic estimation of the cross-covariance kernel with temporal delay between hormones — neglected by all prior periprandial analyses — is what encodes the inter-hormonal interactions with temporal causality that constitute the dynamic wiring of the entero-pancreatic axis. This methodological contribution is reusable beyond the FDEP-TP framework.
