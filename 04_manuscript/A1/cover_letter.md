I am pleased to submit *Multivariate functional principal component eigenfunctions of the entero-pancreatic system: conceptual and physiological foundations of the FDEP-TP framework for periprandial phenotype characterisation* as an Original Research Article for consideration in *The Lancet Diabetes & Endocrinology*.

**Physiological gap.** The entero-pancreatic axis behaves as a coupled secretion–inhibition oscillator on the periprandial transition. Distal L-cells, proximal K-cells, pancreatic islets, and gastric ghrelin sources have each been described in isolation, yet the joint geometry that couples them across the periprandial window has not been decomposed into its eigen-axes. The Ahlqvist, Udler, and Tübingen taxonomies classify individuals using static cross-sectional features and discard the temporal geometry of the periprandial response.

**Lancet D&E precedent.** Madsbad's 2014 mechanism review (Lancet D&E 2:152) and the 2025 clinical-obesity commission (Rubino et al. 13:221) articulate a journal tradition of mechanism-to-algorithm translation that the present article extends to the joint multi-hormonal panel.

**Contribution.** We interpret the four leading mFPC eigenfunctions of the joint enteropancreatic operator in physiological terms. From a pre-registered ecological corpus of 23 studies and 2,750 pseudo-subjects, the joint Happ–Greven operator retained K=12 components (Golovkine 2025 multivariate-FVE ≥ 0·90; cumulative 92 %; N/K=29·2). The first eigenfunction is **PYY-dominant** (‖·‖² = 0·762) with secondary integrated incretin contribution 0·211 — capturing distal L-cell variation rather than incretin-amplitude contrast. The second recovers **proximal-versus-distal entero-endocrine sequencing** along the small bowel as a continuous geometric axis. The third captures **biphasic glucose–insulin coupling**; the fourth, **ghrelin tone**, with a distinctive sleeve-gastrectomy signature. Persistent dysphysiological signalling — not uniform normalisation — characterises post-bariatric and post-caloric-restriction states on the four-axis decomposition.
