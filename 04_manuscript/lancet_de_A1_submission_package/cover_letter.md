I am pleased to submit *Multivariate functional principal component eigenfunctions of the entero-pancreatic system: conceptual and physiological foundations of the FDEP-TP framework for periprandial phenotype characterisation* as an Original Research Article for consideration in *The Lancet Diabetes & Endocrinology*.

**Physiological gap.** The entero-pancreatic axis behaves as a coupled secretion–inhibition oscillator on the periprandial transition. Distal L-cells, proximal K-cells, pancreatic islets, and gastric ghrelin sources have each been described in isolation, yet the joint geometry that couples them across the periprandial window has not been decomposed into its eigen-axes. The Ahlqvist five-cluster, Udler soft-genetic, and Tübingen prediabetes taxonomies classify individuals using static cross-sectional features and discard the temporal geometry that distinguishes a brisk first-phase incretin pulse from a delayed, blunted, or biphasic response.

**Lancet Diabetes & Endocrinology precedent.** Madsbad's 2014 mechanism review (Lancet D&E 2:152) and the 2025 Lancet D&E clinical-obesity commission (Rubino et al. 13:221) articulate a journal tradition of mechanism-to-algorithm translation. Frøslie's functional analysis of glucose curves (BMC Med Res Methodol 2013) opens the methodological line that the present article extends to the joint multi-hormonal panel.

**Contribution.** We interpret the four leading mFPC eigenfunctions of the joint enteropancreatic operator in physiological terms. From a pre-registered ecological corpus of 23 studies and 2,750 pseudo-subjects, the joint Happ–Greven operator retained K=12 components at the Golovkine 2025 multivariate-FVE ≥ 0·90 threshold (cumulative FVE 92 %; N/K=29·2). The first eigenfunction is **PYY-dominant** (‖·‖² = 0·762) with a secondary integrated incretin contribution of 0·211 — capturing distal L-cell variation rather than the classical incretin-amplitude contrast. The second recovers the **anatomical proximal-versus-distal sequencing** along the small bowel as a continuous geometric axis. The third captures **biphasic glucose–insulin coupling**. The fourth captures **ghrelin tone**, with a distinctive sleeve-gastrectomy signature consistent with fundic resection. Cohort-level qualitative signatures across health, T2DM, caloric restriction, and metabolic surgery emerge from the four-axis decomposition: persistent dysphysiological signalling — not uniform normalisation — characterises post-bariatric and post-caloric-restriction states.
