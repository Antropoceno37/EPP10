Dear Editors,

I am pleased to submit *Methodological and translational foundations of the FDEP-TP framework: PACE-mFPCA pipeline, PTP/IEP classification, and clinical implications across obesity, type 2 diabetes, and metabolic surgery* as an Original Research Article for consideration in *The Lancet Diabetes & Endocrinology*.

**Clinical translational gap.** Existing data-driven taxonomies of metabolic disease — Ahlqvist five-cluster, Udler soft-genetic, Tübingen prediabetes — classify individuals using static cross-sectional variables and do not generate clinically actionable algorithms keyed to multi-hormonal periprandial dynamics. The present article closes this gap by formalising the Periprandial Type Profile and Integrated Enteropancreatic Pattern hierarchy with explicit eight-rule precedence and three-suffix glycaemic context, and by operationalising it with a sixteen-script reproducible pipeline.

**Lancet D&E precedent.** Madsbad 2014 (Lancet D&E 2:152), the 2025 clinical-obesity commission (Rubino 13:221), and Lobato 2025 articulate a tradition of mechanism-to-algorithm translation. Heise 2022 (Lancet D&E 10:418), Frías 2021 (NEJM 385:503), and Rosenstock 2023 (NEJM 389:514) provide pharmacological context for the dual and triple-receptor agonists. The present integrated framework supplies the keyed algorithm these strands invite.

**Contribution.** Three signatures emerge from the PTP/IEP classification of 2,750 pseudo-subjects across seven cohorts: (i) **Type V is bariatric-dominant rather than RYGBP-exclusive** — 43·3 % of RYGBP and 30·7 % of SG, both Bonferroni-adjusted permutation q=0·035; (ii) **Type III dominates obesity+T2DM (40·0 %) and post-caloric-restriction (36·8 %)**, reframing partial glycaemic remission as persistent infra-physiological signalling; (iii) **Type I·I dominates the non-obese-without-T2D reference** (32·2 %), with 23·1 % structural not-classifiable from single-analyte source arms. A clinical-decision algorithm keyed on Type and glycaemic suffix is presented (Table 4 + DI4), including the "Tipo VI" extension for triple-receptor agonists.
