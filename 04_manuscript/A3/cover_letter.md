**Contribution.** Three signatures emerge from the PTP/IEP classification: (i) Type V (enhanced–mixed with residual dysglycaemia) is bariatric-dominant rather than RYGBP-exclusive — 43·3 % of RYGBP and 30·7 % of SG, statistically separated from the non-obese without T2D reference at Bonferroni q=0·035; (ii) Type III (infra-physiological) dominates obesity+T2DM (40·0 %) and caloric restriction (36·8 %), reframing partial glycaemic remission as persistent infra-physiological signalling; (iii) Type I·I dominates the non-obese without T2D reference (32·2 %), with 23·1 % not-classifiable arising from single-analyte source arms (a structural, not statistical, consequence). A clinical-decision algorithm keyed on Type and glycaemic suffix is presented, including the "Tipo VI" extension for triple-receptor agonists (retatrutide).

**Position within a coherent trilogy.** Companion paper A2 (Lancet D&E, branch `lancet-A2-mathematical`, tag `A2-submitted-2026-05-09`) supplies the mathematical and statistical layer; companion paper A1 supplies the conceptual-physiological layer.
